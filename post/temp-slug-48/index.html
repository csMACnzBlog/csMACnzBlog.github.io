Lets set the scene: > Bacon Traders is a company who on sell meat atcompetitiveprices in the local shop in their small town. A large part of this has become taking phone orders from nearby towns and the big city down the road. Toaccommodatethose customers who are unable to pick up their order, a delivery service (for a small fee) was started, and has become a popular service to the point where they are regularly delivering to the entire region. A few of their customers have suggested anon-lineweb sales channel would reach a wider audience, and so a e-commerce site has been commissioned to extend on their ever-growing business.

This is where we are, and where our wireframing begins. Now we have some context, lets get into it shall we? A good way to start off when designing an application or new screens, is to use pen and paper. I find that a few leafs of paper can quickly filter through some of the larger details and make the move to a digital version quicker and smoother. This reduces the amount of time spent staring and the screen and thinking about what goes next, and a quicker way to jot down ideas with a client as you flush out the details. It also means your not focussing too much on presentation and getting everything looking just so (if you are as pedantic as I can be this is very important!), its only a rough draft of the draft you will be showing off to others and building off of.
