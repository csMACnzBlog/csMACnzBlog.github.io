At a recent conference the topic of the Hero Developer came up. It got me thinking and connected together a few dots that I would like to put forward to you now.

Hero Developer is not a person, but instead is a valuable role on a team that is a positive asset.

Mobbing and the Hypermind

There is real power in a consistent style and approach to building a piece of, or collection of software.

A role, not a person

It follows that if we look at the Hypermind developed in a team, and the actions behind the team representing the positive benefits achieved from a Hero Developer, that in fact it is best to look at it as a Role, filled by this hypermind and not as a person.

This role, for a given team, can be picked up and fulfilled by any or all members of a team at the same time or at different times.

I think this plays nicely into many of the Agile principles of teams and roles and shared responsibilities. We can still have technical experts and specialities, but we work as a single unified whole. If that whole can sustain with a Hero Developer then it can gain all the advantages from it. If it stays a role rather than a person, the team as a collective can also help combat the negative aspects or side affects normally associated with the Hero Developer.
